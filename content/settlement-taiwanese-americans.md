# Taiwanese American Settlement Patterns

> **Source:** Wikipedia - Taiwanese Americans  
> **Extracted:** 2026-05-12  
> **Data:** 2024 ACS 5-year estimates

## Population Growth

- **2000**: 144,795 Taiwanese Americans
- **2010**: 230,382 Taiwanese Americans (+59%)
- **2013**: 68% born outside US, 67% of foreign-born are naturalized citizens
- **2024**: Record 137,000 Taiwanese went overseas to work in the US

## Key Settlement Areas

### California Communities
- **Bay Area**: Cupertino, San Jose, Fremont, Newark, Pleasanton, Palo Alto, San Mateo, Dublin
- **Southern California**: Arcadia, San Marino, Temple City, South Pasadena, Hacienda Heights, Rowland Heights, Walnut, Diamond Bar
- **Monterey Park**: Historic center, still hosts annual Taiwanese cultural events during Taiwanese Heritage Week

### New York Communities
- **Flushing, Queens**: Main Taiwanese cultural, commercial, and political center in NYC
- **Forest Hills**: Major Taiwanese community
- **Nassau County**: Large Taiwanese population
- **Jersey City & Hoboken, NJ**: Significant concentrations

### Other Major Centers
- **Houston, TX**: Bellaire Boulevard area (new Chinatown)
- **Bellevue, WA**: Greater Seattle "Eastside"
- **Rockville, MD**: Northwest of Washington D.C.
- **Sugar Land, TX**: Near Houston
- **Plano, TX**: Near Dallas
- **Chandler, AZ**: Near Phoenix
- **Atlanta, GA**: Northeastern suburbs

## Settlement Patterns Notes

- Most communities formed after 1965 Immigration and Nationality Act
- School district prestige and high-tech careers heavily influence settlement choices
- Flushing, Queens reflects new investments from Taiwan into newer enclaves vs traditional Chinatowns
- Monterey Park was historic center but no longer largest in LA County
- TSMC Arizona megaproject driving new migration to Chandler area
