# Dr. Albert S. Lai (賴信雄)

**Dr. Albert S. Lai (賴信雄)** is a **Taiwanese American** historian and scholar, recognized as one of the pioneers in establishing Taiwanese American Studies as an academic field.

## Biography

- **Chinese name:** 賴信雄
- **Romanized name:** Lai Hsin-Hsiung / Lai Xinxiong
- **Academic title:** Professor Emeritus of History
- **Institution:** University of Hawaii at Manoa

## Academic Contributions

Dr. Lai was instrumental in establishing **Taiwanese American Studies** as a recognized academic discipline. His scholarship helped bring attention to the often-overlooked history of Taiwanese Americans within the broader Asian American narrative.

### Research Focus

- **Taiwanese immigration to Hawaii** — Early Taiwanese settlements and agricultural communities
- **Taiwanese American community history** — Formation and evolution of diaspora communities
- **228 Incident (二二八事件)** — Its impact on Taiwanese diaspora communities and identity formation
- **Cultural identity** — Taiwanese American identity and cultural preservation

### Academic Positions

- Professor Emeritus of History, **University of Hawaii at Manoa**
- Contributor to the broader field of **Asian American Studies**

## Publications

Dr. Lai has authored and co-authored numerous academic papers and books on Taiwanese American history. His work is cited in broader studies of Taiwanese American demographics, immigration patterns, and cultural identity.

## Significance

Dr. Lai's work fills a critical gap in the historiography of Asian American experiences. Before scholars like him, Taiwanese American history was often subsumed under broader "Chinese American" categories, despite the distinct political, cultural, and linguistic identity of Taiwanese immigrants.
