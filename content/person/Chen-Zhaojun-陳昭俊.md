## NATPA South California Chapter

- One of the **5 founding members** of the NATPA (North America Taiwanese Professors' Association) South California Chapter, established **1985**
- The Ad Hoc Committee consisted of: [[person/Chen-Zhaojun-陳昭俊]], [[person/Jen-Chinbiao-簡錦標]], [[person/Ho-Yuhhsia-何汝諧]], [[person/Hsieh-Chingchih-謝清志]], [[person/Wang-Chiusen-王秋森]]
- Elected **Vice President** of the S Cal Chapter at the founding meeting
- [[person/Ho-Yuhhsia-何汝諧]] served as the first President; [[person/Hsieh-Chingchih-謝清志]] as Secretary/Treasurer

## Founding Ceremony

- **Date:** December 14, 1985
- **Location:** Lincoln Plaza Hotel, Monterey Park ("Little Taipei")
- 15 founding members attended (26 total including spouses)

## NATPA Context

- NATPA founded on February 16, 1980, at the University of Chicago
- The S Cal Chapter grew from 15 founding members to nearly 100 — becoming NATPA's largest and most active chapter among its 11 regional chapters
- At the time of the chapter's establishment (1985), there were **45 active Taiwanese associations/organizations** in the greater Los Angeles area
- The chapter established a "Speakers Bureau" and later launched the "S Cal Tribune" (南加學壇), publishing weekly articles in 國際日報 and 太平洋時報

## Community Connections

- Early supporter of Pastor Chang's pioneering church work in the Los Angeles Taiwanese American community
